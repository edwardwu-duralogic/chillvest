Product Overview

ChillVest is a wearable personal cooling system that stores cold energy in flexible gel-filled cells sewn into the vest lining and releases it steadily against the body over several hours. The result is a measurable reduction in skin temperature and perceived heat load — without ice, dripping water, or bulky cooling hardware.

Designed for workers and individuals regularly exposed to temperatures above 35°C / 95°F, ChillVest fits naturally into existing workwear and safety protocols. It is certified for European markets and is actively pursuing US regulatory clearances (FCC, CPSC) ahead of a North American commercial launch.

How It Works

1

Pre-Cool

Place coldbank cells in a standard freezer or ice cooler for 1–2 hours before the shift.

2

Wear

Put on the vest. Gel cells sit against the body, separated by a thin comfort fabric layer.

3

Sustained Cooling

Body heat conducts into the cold gel, which absorbs it evenly — no cold spike, steady release over hours.

4

Recharge

When gel reaches ambient temperature, return cells to freezer or cooler for the next shift.

5

Fluid Circulation

USB-powered DC 5V motor circulates coolant fluid through the vest, ensuring even temperature distribution across all cooling zones.

CB2026 Coldbank — Chemical Composition

The CB2026 coolant is a proprietary two-ingredient gel optimized for maximum thermal capacity, body-safe handling, and flexibility at sub-zero storage temperatures.

Ingredient CAS No. Proportion Function
Sodium polyacrylate 9003-04-7 50% Superabsorbent polymer — forms gel matrix, holds large thermal mass, stays solid if a cell is punctured (no spill hazard).
Propylene glycol 57-55-6 50% Food-grade antifreeze — prevents mixture from freezing rigid, keeps cells pliable and comfortable against the body.
